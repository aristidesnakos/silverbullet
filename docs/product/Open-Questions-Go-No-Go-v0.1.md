# Lodestar — Open Questions & Go/No-Go
### What we decide ourselves, what needs others, and whether to start · v0.1 · June 2026

## Verdict: start M0 now
The roadmap is good enough to *start*, not to *commit the whole build* — by design. It's a de-risking ladder: each gate dies cheap or earns the next. Starting M0 (the greybox verb test) needs almost nothing resolved first, costs ~3–5 days, and answers the single most fundamental question we own. **Starting ≠ committing.** The real risk today is over-planning instead of feeling the verb.

## Questions WE must answer ourselves
*We have everything needed. Waiting on others here is avoidance.*
- **Is the core verb fun?** Only M0 answers it. The kill gate. Nobody else can answer this.
- **The one sentence the player leaves with.** Working version: *"drive the medicine to the exact spot instead of poisoning the whole body — and it's real."*
- **Tone.** Hopeful, dignified, never arcade "blast the tumour."
- **Scope discipline.** What we refuse to build (no game-depth, no clinical-sim creep). Anti-audience is the guardrail.
- **Aesthetic direction.** Calm, Osmos-grade look.
- **Investment box.** Time/money before external validation + the kill criterion we'll honor.
- **Skills audit.** Can solo + AI carry M0–M2? Where do we want a collaborator? (Most likely the M2 art/shader pass.)
- **Founder fit.** Do we want this to be the project?
- **Success metrics.** Thresholds for completion %, comprehension, share rate.

## The sequencing rule
**Answer every *internal* question by building (M0 → M1). Defer every *external* ask until there's a slice to show — "build then pitch."** A playable link beats ten pitch decks.
