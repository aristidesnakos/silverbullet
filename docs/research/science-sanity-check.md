## Are the mechanisms accurate?
- **(a) Gradient steering, F = ∇(m·B) — correct but incomplete.** A field *gradient* gives translational pull; a *uniform* field gives only torque (rotation). Our "gradient pulls, direction doesn't" framing is right for a *passive dragged blob*. **But** ETH's famous helical microrobots are **torque-driven** (a rotating field screws a chiral body forward), and torque scales with field magnitude, which penetrates tissue better than steep gradients. Keep gradient-pull as *one* legitimate mechanism — don't imply it's *the* mechanism.
- **(b) Low-Reynolds / overdamped — correct.** Viscosity-dominated, inertia negligible (Purcell scallop-theorem regime). State plainly; it adds credibility.
- **(c) Magnetic hyperthermia release — real and demonstrated.** An AC field (~tens–hundreds of kHz) heats iron-oxide nanoparticles (SPIONs); heat-triggered release from thermosensitive gels is well-established in vitro and in animals. Note the steer field and the heat field are *different regimes* — our game already separates "steer" from "charge to heat," which is correct.
- **(d) Gel/shell dissolution release — accurate as one mechanism** (thermal). Others exist (pH, enzymatic, degradation). Fine to portray.

**Net:** nothing is *wrong*. The one oversimplification: real systems often steer by torque/propulsion, not pure gradient-pull. Our passive-blob simplification is defensible if labelled "one way," not "the way."

## Disease scenarios
- **Stroke clot-buster — strongest.** Large accessible high-flow vessels; active preclinical area; high need. Keep, frame preclinical.
- **Deep infection antibiotic — mild stretch on "deep."** More credible for accessible cavities (bladder, GI). **Soften** to "hard-to-reach infection."
- **Brain tumour chemo — biggest stretch.** Blood–brain barrier + deep steering-force hurdles. Aligns with the hyperthermia/NanoTherm story, so not absurd, but frame as a *long-term goal*, not a current capability — or swap to a more accessible tumour.

## Defensible vs. avoid
**Defensible (with "emerging / animal-stage" framing):** "magnetic fields can steer tiny particles and heat iron-oxide to release a drug"; "at this scale blood is like thick syrup" (low-Re); "magnetic hyperthermia is already used in some cancer treatments"; "targeted delivery *aims to* reduce dose to healthy tissue"; "researchers at ETH and elsewhere are developing magnetically steered microrobots" (verify the specific quote).

**Avoid:** present-tense cure/efficacy ("cures cancer," "dissolves clots in patients," "beats chemo" → use *aims to / could / in animal studies*); "doctors use microrobots in your body today"; implying gradient-pull is the only/easy way to go deep; specific numbers without a citation; quoting the ETH article verbatim until confirmed.

## Fatal-flaw check
**No fatal flaw.** The realest stressor: magnetic *gradient* force falls off steeply with distance, so pure gradient-pulling a small particle deep in fast blood is weak vs. drag. Not categorically fatal — mitigated by torque/propulsion, swarms, accessible/low-flow targets, and clinical coil systems. Only kills the *naive* "one particle, gradient-pulled, deep in fast blood" framing — which the game sidesteps by showing flow as a real adversary and not claiming arbitrary depth.
